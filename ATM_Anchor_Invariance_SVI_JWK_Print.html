SVI-JWK Validation Note

ATM Anchor and ATM Invariance

Analytical derivation and numerical sanity-check framework for validating the ATM identity, parameter invariance, curvature behaviour, and cross-maturity consistency of the SVI-JWK volatility surface formulation.

ATM Anchor and ATM Invariance — Full Analysis

Executive Overview

The validation observation has two distinct but related parts:

ATM Anchor: When $\nu_t$ changes, the ATM implied volatility should move in a precisely quantifiable way — the model must be anchored correctly at the money.

ATM Invariance: When $\psi_t$, $\kappa_t$, $p_t$, $c_t$ change, the ATM implied volatility should remain exactly unchanged — these parameters control smile shape, not level.

Both claims are analytically derivable from the SVI-JWK equations and should be numerically verified in the sanity check. Neither was done in the prior validation.

Part 1 — Analytical Derivation

Starting Point — ATM Implied Volatility in SVI-JWK

ATM means log-forward moneyness $k = 0$, i.e. $K = F_0$.

From the raw SVI total variance formula (equation 4.1):

$$w(k; \chi_R) = a + b\left\{\rho(k-m) + \sqrt{(k-m)^2 + \sigma^2}\right\}$$

At $k = 0$:

$$w(0; \chi_R) = a + b\left\{-\rho m + \sqrt{m^2 + \sigma^2}\right\}$$

The ATM Black-Scholes implied volatility is:

$$\sigma_{ATM}(t) = \sqrt{\frac{w(0; \chi_R)}{t}}$$

Now substitute the SVI-JWK expressions for $a$, $b$, $m$, $\sigma$, $\rho$ from equations (4.3)–(4.12). From equation (4.3) rearranged:

$$a = \nu_t t - b\left\{-\rho m + \sqrt{m^2 + \sigma^2}\right\}$$

Substituting directly into $w(0)$:

$$w(0) = \underbrace{\nu_t t - b\left\{-\rho m + \sqrt{m^2 + \sigma^2}\right\}}_{a} + b\left\{-\rho m + \sqrt{m^2 + \sigma^2}\right\}$$

The $b\{...\}$ terms cancel exactly:

$$\boxed{w(0; \chi_R) = \nu_t \cdot t}$$

Therefore:

$$\boxed{\sigma_{ATM}(t) = \sqrt{\frac{\nu_t \cdot t}{t}} = \sqrt{\nu_t}}$$

This Single Result Delivers Everything

This is the fundamental ATM identity of SVI-JWK. It states that:

$$\sigma_{ATM} = \sqrt{\nu_t}$$

always — regardless of the values of $\psi_t$, $\kappa_t$, $p_t$, $c_t$.

This immediately gives us both properties:

ATM Anchor — The $\nu_t$ Relationship

$$\sigma_{ATM} = \sqrt{\nu_t} \implies \nu_t = \sigma_{ATM}^2$$

$\nu_t$ is literally the squared ATM implied volatility. This is the economic interpretation of $\nu_t$ — it is not an abstract parameter but a directly observable market quantity.

Analytical prediction for the sanity check:

A perturbation $\Delta\nu_t$ in ATM variance produces:

$$\Delta\sigma_{ATM} = \frac{\partial\sigma_{ATM}}{\partial\nu_t}\Delta\nu_t = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\nu_t}}\Delta\nu_t = \frac{\Delta\nu_t}{2\sigma_{ATM}}$$

In relative terms:

$$\frac{\Delta\sigma_{ATM}}{\sigma_{ATM}} = \frac{\Delta\nu_t}{2\nu_t}$$

So a 10% increase in $\nu_t$ should produce exactly a 5% increase in $\sigma_{ATM}$ — a precisely quantifiable, analytically derivable relationship. A robust sanity check should not merely show ATM volatility shifting upward with $\nu_t$; it should verify that the magnitude of the shift is analytically correct.

ATM Invariance — The Four Parameters

Since $w(0) = \nu_t t$ identically, and $\nu_t$ appears nowhere in the expressions for $b$, $\rho$, $m$, $\sigma$ except through $a$ (which cancels), we have:

$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{ATM}}{\partial\psi_t} = 0$$
$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{ATM}}{\partial\kappa_t} = 0$$
$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{ATM}}{\partial p_t} = 0$$
$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{ATM}}{\partial c_t} = 0$$

Exactly zero. Not approximately zero, not negligible — exactly zero analytically. These four parameters control smile shape (rotation, curvature, wing steepness) but are completely decoupled from ATM level by construction.

Let us verify this explicitly for $\psi_t$ (skew) to make it concrete.

Verification for $\psi_t$:

From the conversion chain, $\psi_t$ enters through $\beta$ (equation 4.8):

$$\beta = \frac{c_t - p_t - 4\psi_t}{c_t + p_t}$$

$\beta$ then feeds into $\gamma$, $m$, $\sigma$, and ultimately $a$. But we showed $w(0) = \nu_t t$ identically — the entire $b\{-\rho m + \sqrt{m^2+\sigma^2}\}$ structure, which contains $\psi_t$ through $m$ and $\sigma$, cancels out exactly in $w(0)$.

Therefore varying $\psi_t$ changes $m$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $a$ — but their combined effect on $w(k=0)$ is identically zero. $\sigma_{ATM}$ is invariant to $\psi_t$ exactly. $\square$

The same argument holds for $\kappa_t$, $p_t$, $c_t$ by identical reasoning — they all enter through $\beta$, $\gamma$, $b$ but cancel at $k = 0$.

ATM Slope — Analytical Expression for Skew Vega

While ATM level is invariant to $\psi_t$, the slope of the smile at ATM is controlled by $\psi_t$. Differentiating $w(k)$ with respect to $k$ at $k=0$:

$$\frac{\partial w}{\partial k}\bigg|_{k=0} = b\left\{\rho + \frac{-m}{\sqrt{m^2+\sigma^2}}\right\}$$

The ATM implied vol slope is:

$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{BS}}{\partial k}\bigg|_{k=0} = \frac{1}{2\sigma_{ATM} t}\frac{\partial w}{\partial k}\bigg|_{k=0}$$

Substituting SVI-JWK expressions and using $w(0) = \nu_t t$:

$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{BS}}{\partial k}\bigg|_{k=0} = \frac{b}{2\sqrt{\nu_t t}}\left\{\rho - \frac{m}{\sqrt{m^2+\sigma^2}}\right\}$$

In the limit $m \rightarrow 0$ (i.e., $\beta \rightarrow 0$):

$$\frac{\partial\sigma_{BS}}{\partial k}\bigg|_{k=0} \approx \frac{b\rho}{2\sqrt{\nu_t t}} = \frac{\sqrt{\nu_t t}(c_t+p_t)}{2 \cdot 2\sqrt{\nu_t t}} \cdot \frac{c_t-p_t}{c_t+p_t} = \frac{c_t-p_t}{4}$$

This shows ATM skew is primarily controlled by $c_t - p_t$ — the asymmetry between call and put wing slopes — not directly by $\psi_t$. The ATM skew $\psi_t$ controls the smile rotation through $\beta$, but the leading-order ATM slope is determined by wing asymmetry. This is a non-obvious result that the sanity check completely misses.

ATM Curvature — Analytical Expression for Kurtosis Vega

The ATM curvature (butterfly sensitivity) is:

$$\frac{\partial^2\sigma_{BS}}{\partial k^2}\bigg|_{k=0} = \frac{1}{2\sigma_{ATM}t}\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial k^2}\bigg|_{k=0} - \frac{1}{4\sigma_{ATM}^3 t^2}\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial k}\bigg|_{k=0}\right)^2$$

The second derivative of $w$ at $k=0$:

$$\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial k^2}\bigg|_{k=0} = \frac{b\sigma^2}{(m^2+\sigma^2)^{3/2}}$$

In the limit $m \rightarrow 0$:

$$\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial k^2}\bigg|_{k=0} \approx \frac{b}{\sigma} = \frac{b}{\sigma}$$

And from equation (4.12):

$$\kappa_t = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{\nu_t}}\left(\psi_t^2 - \frac{b\sigma^2}{2(m^2+\sigma^2)^{3/2}}\right)$$

Rearranging (at $m = 0$, $\psi_t \approx 0$):

$$\frac{b}{\sigma} \approx 4\sqrt{\nu_t}\kappa_t$$

Therefore:

$$\frac{\partial^2\sigma_{BS}}{\partial k^2}\bigg|_{k=0} \approx \frac{4\sqrt{\nu_t}\kappa_t}{2\sqrt{\nu_t} \cdot t} = \frac{2\kappa_t}{t}$$

This is the analytical prediction: ATM curvature scales as $2\kappa_t/t$ — inversely proportional to maturity (smiles flatten at long maturities) and linearly proportional to $\kappa_t$. The June 2022 redefinition of $\kappa_t \rightarrow \kappa_t/\sqrt{t}$ means this becomes $2\kappa_t^{\text{new}}/\sqrt{t}$ — decaying as $1/\sqrt{t}$, which is the more natural term structure behaviour.

Part 3 — Why These Tests Matter for the Prior Validation Specifically

Implication 1 — Implementation Verification

The ATM identity $w(0) = \nu_t t$ involves an exact cancellation of the $b\{-\rho m + \sqrt{m^2+\sigma^2}\}$ terms between $a$ and $w(0)$. In a floating-point implementation, this cancellation may not be exact — particularly in regimes where $m$ is large (near-singular regions) and the two terms being subtracted are large numbers of similar magnitude (catastrophic cancellation).

A numerical test of ATM invariance directly checks whether the implementation achieves this cancellation to sufficient precision across the full parameter range.

Implication 2 — The Smoothness Issue in §5.10

The non-smooth Kurtosis Vega observed in §5.10 is a downstream manifestation of the curvature formula $\partial^2\sigma_{BS}/\partial k^2|_{k=0} \approx 2\kappa_t/t$. If the ATM curvature is non-smooth as a function of $\kappa_t$ at the system level, it means either:

  • The analytical relationship $\approx 2\kappa_t/t$ is not holding numerically — pointing to an implementation issue in how the downstream pricing model uses the SVI-JWK surface
  • The relationship holds at the SVI-JWK level but is distorted by the downstream equity swap pricing model

Testing ATM curvature analytically against its predicted value $2\kappa_t/t$ at the SVI-JWK level — before passing to the downstream model — would isolate which layer introduces the non-smoothness.

Implication 3 — The >1bp PnL Finding (§5.14)

The unexplained PnL in §5.14 was attributed to missing risk sensitivities for $p_t$ and $c_t$. But the ATM invariance property shows $\partial\sigma_{ATM}/\partial p_t = 0$ and $\partial\sigma_{ATM}/\partial c_t = 0$ exactly. This means the PnL from $p_t$ and $c_t$ changes comes entirely from off-ATM Greeks — the sensitivity of OTM option prices to wing slope changes. The risk system's failure to capture this is precisely the failure to compute the smile-shape Greeks $\partial\sigma_{BS}(k)/\partial p_t$ for $k \neq 0$ and $\partial\sigma_{BS}(k)/\partial c_t$ for $k \neq 0$ — while correctly computing zero for $k = 0$. The ATM invariance test would have made this gap analytically explicit.
